# Code of Conduct

The DSR Framework project expects professional, constructive, and evidence-oriented participation.

## Expected Behavior

- Treat contributors, reviewers, and users respectfully.
- Critique ideas, evidence, claims, and implementation details rather than people.
- Make uncertainty explicit.
- Keep discussion relevant to the framework, DSR methodology, repository quality, or publication readiness.
- Respect privacy, source restrictions, and licensing constraints.

## Unacceptable Behavior

- Harassment, threats, personal attacks, or discriminatory language.
- Publishing private information without consent.
- Introducing credentials, restricted source material, or confidential review content into public files.
- Repeatedly asserting unsupported claims after evidence gaps have been identified.

## Enforcement

Maintainers may edit, hide, or remove inappropriate contributions and may restrict participation when conduct interferes with constructive review or responsible publication.

This draft policy should be replaced with a fully adopted conduct policy before public community contribution is invited.
